12-Week BSFL Business Launch Plan - Bleiblerville, TX

This plan outlines a day-by-day roadmap to start a black soldier fly larvae (BSFL) business in Bleiblerville, TX, targeting poultry, pet, and garden markets. Track task status using dropdowns.

Week 1: Research and Legal Foundations

DayTaskResponsibleStatus
1 Review Texas Business Licenses & Permits Guide; identify sales tax permit, assumed name certificate ($25), zoning needs.
2 Contact Austin County AgriLife Extension (979-865-2072) for BSFL intro call; attend virtual webinar.
3 Research local waste sources; visit 2-3 farms/dairies for feedstock partnerships.
4 Map buyers: List 10 poultry ops/pet stores in Bleiblerville/Brenham; confirm demand via calls.
5 Apply preliminarily for USDA UAIP grant ($250K max) via grants.gov.
6 File assumed name with Austin County Clerk ($25); open business bank account.
7 Rest/review: Compile notes into one-page summary.

Week 2: Business Planning and Market Fit

DayTaskResponsibleStatus
1 Draft business plan (SBA template); project $1,000-5,000/month revenue from larvae/frass.
2 Calculate costs; budget $2,000 for starter larvae/eggs from Symton BSF (College Station).
3 Identify 5 pilot sites (e.g., Bleiblerville Community Garden, Hope Farms Houston).
4 Apply for sales tax permit (comptroller.texas.gov).
5 Join TX Backyard Chickens Facebook group; post query on BSFL interest.
6 Refine targets; focus on pet/reptile market (e.g., Gecko World Austin).
7 Rest/review: Finalize 1-year projection (IRR 25-40%).

Week 3: Site and Equipment Setup

DayTaskResponsibleStatus
1 Scout property; secure 500-1,000 sq ft (backyard/rented barn).
2 Order plastic bins ($200), substrate, climate tools ($300) from Amazon/Home Depot.
3 Build prototype bin (4x4 ft; Texas A&M guide).
4 Secure waste supply; sign MOUs with 2 local sources.
5 Install shade cloth, basic irrigation ($100).
6 Test setup; run water/ventilation dry run (target 60-75% humidity).
7 Rest/review: Inventory checklist complete.

Week 4: Sourcing and Initial Stocking

DayTaskResponsibleStatus
1 Order 1,000 BSFL eggs from Symton BSF ($50-100).
2 Source colony flies; buy 100 adults (e.g., Fluker Farms).
3 Prep feedstock; collect/test 100 lbs waste (scraps/manure).
4 Set up breeding chamber (dark, warm box; $50 DIY).
5 Apply for SARE On-Farm Research Grant ($30K max).
6 Train: Watch 3 YouTube tutorials on BSFL cycles.
7 Rest/review: All materials arrived.

Week 5: Pilot Production Launch

DayTaskResponsibleStatus
1 Introduce eggs/larvae to bins; monitor temp (77-95°F).
2 Feed initial batch (2x body weight daily); log weights.
3 Check for issues (e.g., moisture); adjust ventilation.
4 Visit local farm for feedback session on potential sales.
5 Document process: Photos/videos for grant apps.
6 Breed first flies (separate pupae).
7 Rest/review: Week 1 cycle metrics.

Week 6: Testing and Iteration

DayTaskResponsibleStatus
1 Harvest first larvae (Day 10-14); dry sample for feed test.
2 Produce frass batch; test soil amendment on garden plot.
3 Analyze costs/yields; tweak feed ratios.
4 Pitch to 3 buyers (e.g., pet store in Brenham); offer free samples.
5 Submit UAIP grant full app (include pilot data).
6 Scale breeding: Add second bin.
7 Rest/review: ROI calc for pilot (target 50% margin).

Week 7: Marketing and Partnerships

DayTaskResponsibleStatus
1 Create website (Wix, $15/month) with product photos/pricing.
2 List on local marketplaces (Facebook, Craigslist); target Bleiblerville/Sealy.
3 Attend Austin County Farm Bureau meeting; network for off-take.
4 Secure first sale: Sell 20 lbs larvae to backyard group.
5 Partner with community garden (e.g., Urban Harvest affiliate).
6 Order modular container basics ($1,000 for small unit).
7 Rest/review: 5 leads tracked.

Week 8: Scale Production

DayTaskResponsibleStatus
1 Launch second cycle; add waste from new partner.
2 Install IoT sensors ($200) for remote monitoring.
3 Process first bulk frass (bag for sales).
4 Deliver samples to 2 farms; collect feedback.
5 File for EIN (irs.gov; free).
6 Train family/helper on harvesting.
7 Rest/review: Yield double check.

Week 9: Sales and Distribution

DayTaskResponsibleStatus
1 Close 2 bulk deals (e.g., pet supplier for $200).
2 Set up delivery logistics (local truck/van).
3 Market frass to landscapers (e.g., via Houzz directory).
4 Track invoices; remit first sales tax (if applicable).
5 Expand to Austin market (reptile events).
6 Apply for TX Dept of Ag grant ($10K+).
7 Rest/review: Cash flow summary.

Week 10: Operations Refinement

DayTaskResponsibleStatus
1 Automate feeding schedule.
2 Audit waste supply; add 1 more source.
3 Test odor controls (ventilation tweaks).
4 Host demo at local garden.
5 Review grant statuses; follow up.
6 Scale to third bin.
7 Rest/review: Monthly P&L.

Week 11: Expansion Prep

DayTaskResponsibleStatus
1 Order full small modular unit ($8,000; assemble).
2 Pitch to 3 community sites for installs.
3 Secure insurance ($500/year for ag liability).
4 Build customer database (20+ contacts).
5 Optimize pricing based on sales data.
6 Plan Q2 goals (e.g., 1 ton/month).
7 Rest/review: Risk assessment.

Week 12: Launch and Monitor

DayTaskResponsibleStatus
1 Full production launch; harvest major batch.
2 Deliver first modular unit (if funded).
3 Celebrate milestone: Invoice $1,000+.
4 Gather testimonials; update marketing.
5 Schedule quarterly AgriLife check-in.
6 Forecast next 3 months; adjust plan.
7 Rest/review: 12-week retrospective; business live!

Track progress daily and adjust as needed. Contact Austin County AgriLife Extension or Symton BSF for support. Aim for 50% margins and 1-2 grant approvals by Week 12.
