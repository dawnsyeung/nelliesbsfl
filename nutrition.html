Feedstock Matrix

Curated Ingredients for Larval Success

We blend pre-consumer produce, spent grains, brewery yeast, pre-treated proteins, and fiber carriers to build a diet that is energy-dense yet digestible. Each ingredient class is paired to deliver complementary nutrients while maintaining consistent moisture and texture profiles for larvae feeding behavior.

Ingredient Class Role in Diet Inclusion Range
Vegetable & Fruit Residues Carbohydrate energy, moisture regulation, vitamin complexes 25–40%
Spent Grains & Bakery Protein backbone, amino acid diversity, fiber structure 20–35%
Fermentation By-Products Live microbes, B-vitamins, lauric acid precursors 10–18%
Oilseed Press Cakes Essential fatty acids, mineral enrichment 8–15%
Fiber & Bulking Agents Chitin balance, gut motility, tray aeration 5–10%

Exclusion Policy

  • No post-consumer meat or dairy
  • No hazardous or pharmaceutical residues
  • No PFAS, PFOS, or persistent contaminants
  • Full allergen tracing with segregation protocols

Operational Practices

Maintaining Larvae Health & Consistency

Diet alone does not guarantee outcomes; we pair nutrition with stewardship practices that protect larvae welfare. Our technicians track behavior, density, and moisture to ensure feed is accessible and digestible across the growth window.

Dynamic feeding schedule responsive to appetite curve data
Moisture control via misting, biochar amendments, and airflow tuning
Larvae health scoring that flags stress signals (clumping, lethargy, off-odors)
Emergency SOPs for temperature excursions or feedstock anomalies

Key Outcomes

  • Consistent amino acid profile for formulations
  • Higher lauric acid for antimicrobial benefits
  • Enhanced chitin content for frass bioactivity
  • Low mortality and uniform larval size
