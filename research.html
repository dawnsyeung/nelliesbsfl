Research on BSFL Waste Valorization, Feed, and Frass Impact

A curated index of peer-reviewed studies and industry field trials demonstrating how Black Soldier Fly systems divert waste, elevate livestock and aquaculture performance, and regenerate soils with biologically active frass.

Research Pillars

Evidence Base for Integrated BSFL Solutions

Organic Waste Valorization

Studies evaluate substrate conversion efficiency, pathogen suppression, and emissions profiles when food waste and manures are processed by larvae.

  • Quantify diversion rates vs. composting and anaerobic digestion
  • Benchmark greenhouse gas savings and biosecure handling
  • Refine feeding regimes for high-moisture waste streams

Animal Nutrition & Health

Larval meal and oil integrations are validated across poultry, swine, aquaculture, and companion animal diets to improve feed conversion, immunity, and product quality.

  • Measure digestibility of proteins, lipids, and functional compounds
  • Track microbiome shifts and gut barrier integrity
  • Compare performance against fishmeal, soybean, and rendered fats

Frass for Soil & Ecology

Field trials and soil biology assessments document how insect frass reintroduces nutrients, microbes, and chitin-derived elicitors that improve plant vigor and resilience.

  • Monitor crop yield and quality responses
  • Evaluate microbial biodiversity and enzymatic activity
  • Assess soil structure, carbon retention, and water use efficiency

Systems & Lifecycle Assessment

Cross-disciplinary work models environmental and economic performance of distributed BSFL infrastructure, informing permitting and ESG reporting for municipal and industrial partners.

  • Compare lifecycle impacts across waste management pathways
  • Map circular value flows for co-products and residues
  • Support investment cases with sensitivity analysis

Peer-Reviewed Studies

Key Findings on Waste, Feed, and Soil Outcomes

Waste Management & Circularity

Demonstrated diversion of food waste and manure with minimal emissions.

  • Diener et al. 2015 – Feeding rates that maximize organic waste conversion by BSFL.
  • Gold et al. 2021 – Environmental assessment of co-managing municipal waste with larvae.
  • Somroo et al. 2021 – BSFL bioconversion performance on mixed organic residues.

Feed Inclusion & Animal Performance

Larval meal supports growth, immunity, and feed efficiency across species.

  • Schiavone et al. 2017 – Broiler tolerance and nutrient uptake from partial BSFL meal replacement.
  • Belghit et al. 2018 – Black soldier fly meal in salmonid diets without compromising growth.
  • Spranghers et al. 2020 – Antimicrobial and immunomodulatory properties in piglet feeding trials.

Frass, Soil Health & Crop Response

Frass delivers nutrients and bioactive compounds that regenerate soils.

  • Beesigamukama et al. 2021 – Maize yield and soil enzyme activity boosted by insect frass fertilization.
  • Quilliam et al. 2020 – Frass improves soil microbial communities and reduces pathogen load.
  • Setti et al. 2022 – Chitin-rich residues enhance plant defense and soil structure.
